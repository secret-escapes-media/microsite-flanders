Find our favourites below, with top tips that will help you get under the skin of truly unique, medieval centres... sections: - id: bruges title: Bruges description: Bruges is a beguiling medieval delight, a chocolate-box town filled with romantic scenes at every turn. It’s one of Europe’s best-preserved, old-world centres, a step-back-in-time, with an indulgent lifestyle to tap into. Under the shadow of the (leaning!) Belfry, head for iconic chocolate shops, discover a maze of medieval streets, marvel at magnificent architecture, take a boat trip along the canal, and prop up the old beer bars after superb fine dining – found in myriad restaurants across this achingly beautiful town. pois: - content: Marvel at the almost hidden Basilica of the Holy Blood, where beautifully painted interiors mark the approach to a remarkable relic... - content: There are 55 chocolate shops to choose from. 55. But one recommendation to heed has to be The Chocolate Line; Dominique Persoone’s shop captures the creative gastro-flair he is so famous for. - content: Head for the Halve Maan Brewery and sip on a Bruges icon – the delicious Brugse Zot. It’s the last family brewhouse in the centre and the beer is unmissable. Proost! - id: ghent title: Ghent description: Ghent is quite possibly one of the finest cities in Europe, yet remains little visited – which makes little sense, as Ghent sure packs a punch. A beguiling medieval centre embellished with intricate facades, spiralling towers and remarkable waterways make Ghent irresistibly beautiful, a picturesque backdrop for waterside dining and discovering quirky Belgian bars. It’s as historic as Bruges, with 15th-century halls lining the ancient wharves, yet the old-world feel belies a youthful heart – a student population thrives here amongst hip bars and coffee hangouts. pois: - content: Discover the spellbinding work, the Adoration of the Mystic Lamb, in the towering Sint-Baafskathedraal – a Van Eyck brothers’ masterpiece. - content: Marvel at the romantic Gothic towers of the imposing Gravensteen Castle, a 12th-century stronghold that was restored in the 19th century with, let’s say, a hint of poetic license! - content: Take a boat around the canals and wharves of the historic centre. It’s a romantic and picturesque way to see the city, with guides unravelling the story of old Ghent... - id: antwerp title: Antwerp description: Antwerp is the Flemish, if not Belgian, capital of cool. Synonymous with daring fashion and university chic, killer clubs and artsy atmosphere, it is – in the same breath – old-school, historic and wonderfully picturesque. Sit outside a cafe looking out over a cobbled square with ancient, stepped-gable houses leaning into each other, or discover the latest Antwerp trend in a boutique around the corner. Museums remind us of Antwerp’s Baroque hero, Rubens; his home is here, as are masterful artworks. Oh, and there are diamonds galore. In short, Antwerp is a city of delicately-balanced contrasts, a kaleidoscope of medieval grandeur, artistic flair and youthful cool. pois: - content: The ornate mansion Rubenshuis, the home of the eponymous Baroque master, is now lovingly restored and filled with rare treasures – art lovers alight! - content: Let Belgium’s most impressive medieval cathedral beguile, with its vast belfry and wealth of Baroque art. The Onze-Lieve-Vrouwekathedraal is a masterpiece of Belgian sacred architecture. - content: The Jane has two Michelin stars and sits in the list of top 100 restaurants in the world. A remarkable setting inside an old chapel, don’t miss this feast for the senses (but be sure to book months in advance!). - id: mechelen title: Mechelen description: Mechelen is a charming town perfectly located between Brussels and Antwerp; consider it somewhat of a hidden gem, a bustling spot for in-the-know travellers. A medieval heart offers much the same romance as the rest of Flanders’ cities, with an impressive collection of historic churches and renowned museums. The pastel-coloured main square is a delight, from which cobbled lanes host cafes and restaurants a-plenty. Don’t miss the nostalgic Toy Museum and one of Belgium’s oldest operating breweries, Anker. pois: - content: Climb the monolithic steeple, St Rumbold's Tower, for astounding panorama over the city; but be warned, the climb consists of a narrow set of 514 steps! - content: Sip Golden Carolus beer from Belgium’s oldest working brewery, Anker. It’s a celebrated taste of Mechelen! - content: Head for the Hof van Busleyden, a stunning Renaissance palace that hosts artworks from Flanders’ Golden Age. - id: leuven title: Leuven description: With one of Europe’s oldest universities at its heart, Leuven is a wonderful balance of old-world charms and youthful exuberance. It is also Belgium’s brewing capital and, quite possibly related, claims the longest bar in the world. You’ll find it in the Oude Markt; it stands for a lengthy stretch of back-to-back bars and cafes, the buzzing centre of Leuven’s social scene. The UNESCO-listed Great Beguinage, best explored by bicycle as it is a little out of the centre of town, is a remarkable collection of lantern-lit streets and medieval structures, intertwined with canals for evocative romance. Leuven’s intricate and ornate 15th-century town hall is quite possibly best observed from a cafe in the square, sipping Leuven’s most famous export, Stella Artois. pois: - content: Marvel at the Stadhuis, a rather extravagant, richly decorated Gothic hall with vast spires that reach into the Leuven skyline. - content: Head for the Old Market Square and alight at one of 45 bars, collectively making the ‘longest bar’ in Europe! - content: Discover the modernist M-Museum, which houses Leuven’s impressive art collection.
