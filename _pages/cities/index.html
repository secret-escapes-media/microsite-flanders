Find our favourites below, with top tips that will help you get under the skin of truly unique, medieval centres... sections: - id: ghent title: Ghent description: Ghent is quite possibly one of the finest cities in Europe, yet remains little visited – which makes little sense, as Ghent sure packs a punch. A captivating medieval centre embellished with intricate facades, spiralling towers and remarkable waterways make Ghent irresistibly beautiful, a picturesque backdrop for waterside dining and discovering quirky Belgian bars. Wonderfully historic, 15th-century halls line ancient wharves, yet the old-world feel belies a youthful heart – a student population thrives here amongst hip bars and coffee hangouts. pois: - content: Discover the spellbinding work, the ‘Adoration of the Mystic Lamb’, in the towering St. Bavo’s Cathedral – a masterpiece created by the Van Eyck brothers. - content: Marvel at the Gothic grandeur of Gravensteen Castle, a 12th-century stronghold that was restored in the 19th century with, let’s say, a romantic vision in mind! - content: Take a boat around the canals and wharves of the historic centre. It’s a romantic and picturesque way to see the city – even better at dusk with the city illuminated – with guides unravelling the story of old Ghent... - id: antwerp title: Antwerp description: Antwerp is the Flemish, if not Belgian, capital of cool. Synonymous with daring fashion and university chic, killer clubs and artsy atmosphere, it is – in the same breath – old-school, historic and wonderfully picturesque. Sit outside a cafe looking out over a cobbled square with ancient, stepped-gable houses standing shoulder-to-shoulder, or discover the latest Antwerp trend in a boutique around the corner. Museums remind us of Antwerp’s Baroque hero, Peter Paul Rubens; his home is here, as are masterful artworks. Oh, and there are diamonds galore – find them in the new DIVA Diamond Museum and in the Diamond Quarter. In short, Antwerp is a city of delicately-balanced contrasts, a kaleidoscope of medieval grandeur, artistic flair and youthful cool. pois: - content: The ornate Rubens House, the home of the eponymous Baroque master, is filled with rare treasures – a must for art lovers! - content: Let Belgium’s most impressive medieval cathedral beguile, with its vast belfry and wealth of Baroque art. The Cathedral of Our Lady is a masterpiece of Belgian sacred architecture. - content: Antwerp has a wealth of Michelin restaurants like Seppe Nobels’ Graanmarkt 13 and The Jane. The latter has two Michelin stars, serving up fine fare inside an old chapel – a rather remarkable experience. - id: mechelen title: Mechelen description: Once the capital of the Burgundian Netherlands, Mechelen is a charming city perfectly located between Brussels and Antwerp; consider it somewhat of a hidden gem, a bustling spot for in-the-know travellers. A medieval heart offers much the same romance as the rest of Flanders’ cities, with an impressive collection of historic churches and renowned museums. The pastel-coloured main square is a delight, from which cobbled lanes host cafes and restaurants a-plenty. Don’t miss the nostalgic Toy Museum and one of Belgium’s oldest operating breweries, Het Anker. pois: - content: Climb the monolithic steeple, St. Rumbold's Tower, for astounding panorama over the city; but be warned, the climb consists of a narrow set of 514 steps! - content: Sip Golden Carolus beer from one of Belgium’s oldest working breweries, Het Anker, in the UNESCO-listed beguinage. It’s a celebrated taste of Mechelen! - content: Head for the Hof van Busleyden, a stunning Renaissance palace adorned with Golden Age artworks and which tells the story of the lesser-known Burgundian period. - id: leuven title: Leuven description: With one of Europe’s oldest universities at its heart, Leuven is a wonderful balance of old-world charms and youthful exuberance. It is also Belgium’s brewing capital and, quite possibly related, claims to have the longest bar in the world. You’ll find it in the Oude Markt; it stands for a lengthy stretch of back-to-back bars and cafes, the buzzing centre of Leuven’s social scene. The UNESCO-listed Great Beguinage, best explored by bicycle as it is a little out of the centre of town, is a remarkable collection of lantern-lit streets and medieval structures, intertwined with canals for evocative romance. Leuven’s intricate and ornate 15th-century town hall is quite possibly best observed from a cafe in the square, sipping Leuven’s most famous export, Stella Artois. pois: - content: Marvel at the Stadhuis, a rather extravagant, richly decorated Gothic town hall with vast spires that reach into the Leuven skyline. - content: Once one of the most prominent abbeys in Belgium, Park Abbey is now filled with exhibitions and polyphonic music year-round in this peaceful part of the city. - content: Discover the modernist M-Museum, which houses Leuven’s impressive art collection.
